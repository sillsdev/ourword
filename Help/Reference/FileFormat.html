OurWord Help
     OurWord Standard Format Markup for Scripture

OurWord stores files using a Standard Format markup structure that is based on Jock Hughes' proposal from the Notes On Computing publication some years ago, and in use by the Timor Team and others in South Asia. The markers have been adjusted to more closely follow the Dallas Translation Department recommendations. The major points to notice are:

  • verse numbers sit in their own field,
  • verse text (or vernacular text) has its own field too,
  • Back translations and translator notes are in the same file as the vernacular translation,
  • back translations of any text field is created by placing a BT in front of the field code,
  • paragraph structure is marked by separate paragraph fields (\p, \m, \q, \q2, etc.)—these do not need to be repeated for BT fields.

Fields

There are a number of field codes. These include the basics, plus fields for introductory material, Back Translation for both "English" and "National". Most fields are set up to give a following field marker. This can be tweaked by the user.

Basic Fields

\id - 3-letter identification code
\h - bookname for the Header
\bth - BT of (h)

\mt - Main Title
\btmt - BT of (mt)
\st - Subtitle
\btst - BT of (st)

\ms - major section
\mr - major section cross reference

\c - Chapter number
\s - Section heading
\bts - BT of (s)
\s2 - secind level section heading
\bts2 - BT of (s2)
\r - cross-Reference or section reference

\p - normal Paragraph
\v - Verse number
\vt - Verse Text (or Vernacular Text)
\btvt - BT of (vt)

Translator Notes

\nt - General Notes
\ntHint - Hints for daughter translations
\ntUns - Things to ask the UNS
\ntReas - The reason or justification for a translation decision
\ntFT - Potential issue in the front translation
\ntDef - Definitions of words
\ntBT - Back Translation information
\ov - Old / previous version of the text
\ntck - Notes for checking
\ntgk - Exegesis note: Greek
\nthb - Exegesis note: Hebrew
\ntcn - Exegesis note: general

Pictures

\cat - Picture catalog number or filename
\ref - Picture reference for size, location
\cap - Picture caption
\btcap - BT of (cap)

Other Common Fields

\q - Quote paragraph
\q2 - 2nd level for a Quote pp
\q3 - 3rd level for a Quote pp
\m - margin paragraph (block or continuation pp)
\hist - Book history
\ud - Toolbox datestamp


Footnotes

Footnotes are handled in the following manner:

\v 1
\vt Tabea fi di yako, Paulus, tongi tu nango roko rua, do, Silas|fn tu Timotius.
\btvt Respectful.greetings from me, Paulus, and also my two friends, [do,] Silas|fn and Timotius.
\ft 1:1: Silas ngaan sahet, do, Silvanus.
\btft 1:1: Silas other name, (do,) Silvanus.

The |fn in both text fields serves to mark the location of the reference. The footnotes follow the sentence (rather than split up the sentence).

Footnotes that attach to the end of a verse or \vt do not require an |fn.

\v 10
\vt Verse text.
\btvt Back translation of verse text.
\ft 4:10: Footnote text. (footnote reference will be placed correctly at end of verse text)
\btft 4:10: Back translation of footnote text.

The routine used for formatting scripture does not handle multiple footnotes in a verse very well. The work-around is to divide the verse into separate bundles as follows.

\v 11
\vt Verse text. (up to first footnote, or through sentence that includes|fn first footnote)
\btvt Back translation of verse text.
\ft 4:11a: First footnote text in verse.
\btft 4:11a: Back translation of footnote text.

\vt Verse text. (rest of verse)
\btvt Back translation of verse text.
\ft 4:11b: Second footnote text in verse. (footnote reference will be placed correctly at end of verse text)
\btft 4:11b: Back translation of footnote text.
