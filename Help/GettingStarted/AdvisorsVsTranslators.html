     Advisors and Translators

OurWord considers users to fit into one of two possible roles:

Indigenous Translators who typically have had little, if any, prior computer experience. They don't need to worry about the complexities of file management, writing systems, installations, and the like. Rather, they want to concentrate on the science and art of Bible translation. OurWord was especially designed to hide software complexity from the translator, in order to enable the inexperienced computer user to quickly become productive and sucessful in his dream of tranlating the Word of God into his own language.

However, complexity must be dealt with, and it is the Advisor who does so. The advisor is the person who makes decisions about how files should be organized; about how the language shoud be keyboarded, or about which tasks the translator is ready to tackle--and it is then the advisor who sets up OurWord to support these decisions.

OurWord places these Adivsor settings into two dialogs, both accessed under the Tools dropdown:

  • Configuration is used to set up all aspects of a translation project
  • Features is used to set up which user interface items are turned on for the translator.

The other areas of the software are expected to be used by the translator.

Of course, some people will fill the roles of both translator and advisor. In fact, we would hope that as translators gain in experience and confidence, that they in turn might become advisors for other translators.
