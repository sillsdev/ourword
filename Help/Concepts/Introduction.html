OurWord Help
     Introduction & Overview

OurWord is editing software designed to support the mother-tongue translator as he translates the Word of God into his own language.

One could argue that any editor would be fine for Bible translation. And indeed if the main requirement is that a person can type and modify text, then software such as Notepad would suffice. But OurWord's purposes include:

  • providing an easy-to-learn, user friendly tool for translators who typically have had little, if any, prior experience using computers,
  • organizing the task in a manner that is most conducive to sucessful Bible translation.

OurWord was initially developed in Timor, Indonesia, to support the method of translation that is done there. The translators in the numberous local languages of Timor draft from the carefully-crafted Kupang Malay translation. Kupang Malay is the language of wider communication in the region. Translators have found that by using the Kupang as a "front" translation, meaning that they follow its exegesis and strategies, that they can quickly produce high-quality translations in their own languages. OurWord was conceived as a means of supporting this process of drafting from a front translation, and the initial version was introduced there in late 2004.

A front translation, by the way, is merely another means of doing adaptation. The Timor translators evaluated more traditional computer adaptation before settling on the "manual adaptation" method that they currently use. (The distinction between computer and manual adaptation is discussed here.)

Having observed that most translators prefer to draft from a single "main" source text (though frequently with access to the Scriptures in other translations for reference purposes,) and having seen that OurWord's presentation has indeed proven effective for newer computer users; OurWord was generalized to support translation projects which do not work from a front translation. The Huichol Old Testament project of Mexico provided the proving ground, as translators drafted Huichol from one of the Spanish translations. And thus OurWord has become a general translation editing tool appropriate for numerous translation situations.

Early versions of Microsoft Word had a feature known as "Side-by-Side Paragraphs." Microsoft discontinued the feature in later versions, but it served as the basis for OurWord's user interface. We place the source paragraph on the left, and a empty space to type the translation of that paragraph on the right. The rest of the software is built upon that foundational notion of editing. You can get a feel for the editing experience by downloading the Walkthrough from the web site, at ourword.theseedcompany.org.

If you are new to OurWord, I recommend that you begin with the walkthrough. This includes doing a translation exercise with the sample data. Once you have completed this you should have a good understanding of the approach that OurWord takes. You might then review the topics under the Getting Started section of the Table of Contents in these helps.
