     Structural Editing

The default mode of operation for OurWord is that the draft will match the source/front translation in terms of structure. Thus if there is a quote paragraph in the front, there will be a quote paragraph in the draft; if a section has three paragraphs in the front, then there will be three corresponding paragraphs in the draft.

While this is a good default means of working during drafting, typically there comes a time where it is desired to make changes. Different paragraph breaks might be desired, or different styles. Often these decisions are made together with a consultant's input, and thus happen later during the translation process.

In order to make these types of changes to the draft, go to the Tools-Setup Features dialog, and turn on Structural Editing. This results in two things:

  1. The Enter and Backspace keys now serve to split or join paragraphs. Thus if you want to add a new paragraph, simply press the Enter key where you want the new paragraph to occur.
  2. The Edit drop-down menu offers a "Change Paragraph To" command, by which you can change the style of the currently selected paragraph.

(We anticipate supporting the insertion and deletion of footnotes in a soon-to-appear future release of OurWord as a part of this Structural Editing feature.)
