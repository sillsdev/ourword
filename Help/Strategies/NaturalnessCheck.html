     Naturalness Check

The Naturalness Check window is a means by which the vernacular text can be read without the clutter of verse numbers, chapter numbers, or the source translation. The idea is that only the translation itself is visible, and the translators or reviewers can read it for naturalness, making sure that the text flows and the proper discourse elements are present.

When verse numbers are present, it is typical for a reader to pause and not see where the text does not flow well. A pitfall of verse-by-verse translating is that the flow of discourse can become abrubt and stilted. We suggest that the translation be examined for naturalness multiple times throughout the translation process; beginning from the completion of the first draft, all the way through the consultant check at the end.

OurWord provides several settings in the Configuration dialog for the Naturalness Check view:

  • While we recommend that verse numbers be turned off, you can elect to turn them on. Some teams find it useful to be able to reference the text via the verse numbers.
  • Similarly, you can turn line numbers on and off. We recommend that they be turned on, so that you can reference the text by numbers that are off to the side, rather than verse numbers which are embedded in the text. Thus a group of people can gather around a computer monitor and say "It really does not flow well in line 32."
